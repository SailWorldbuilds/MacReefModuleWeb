MacArthur Reef Resource Point Calculator

This calculator is intended to help you plan and execute Resource Allocation for MacArthur Reef. All habitats begin Resource Allocation with 100 Resource Points (RP), which their teams may spend freely on the modules found below. Unspent Species Credits (SC) from the Species Selection stage will automatically be converted to RP. You are responsible for ensuring your RP expenditure does not exceed your combined 100 RP and leftover SC.

Structure Modules

Habitat Length & Radius

Adjust the dimensions of your habitat.
The default MacArthur Reef habitat is a 500-kilometer-long, 16-kilometer-radius cylinder.
Habitat area is capped at 50,265.48 km², but can be reduced to a minimum of 12,566.37 km². Smaller habitats will be conferred an RP bonus.
Sliders will automatically update to respect the maximum possible area.

Surface Area: km²

Visualization of Habitat Dimensions

This represents the unwrapped lateral surface of a cylinder, i.e. the livable portion of the habitat. These are the dimensions that should be used for mapping. Please be aware that the top and bottom of the rectangle are a lateral seam and connect to each other.

Habitat Wall Thickness

Adjust the thickness of your habitat's walls.
Thicker habitat walls can confer greater strength and can support deeper water depths and taller artificial mountain peaks, though larger habitat radii reduce this.

Maximum Water Depth

Adjust the maximum depth of oceans and lakes.

  • Water depth determines the highest available tier of the Geothermal Terraforming module.

Maximum Substrate Depth

Adjust the maximum depth of substrate available for terrestrial ecosystems and sediment for aquatic ecosystems.

  • Substrate depth determines the highest available tier of the Subterranean Terraforming module.

Maximum Elevation

Adjust the maximum elevation of terrain above sea level. This raises the sintered stone bedrock to increase average land height for the creation of prominent mountains, deep valleys, and rugged terrain.

  • Elevation determines the highest available tier of the Hydrological Terraforming module.

Habitat End Caps

Adjust the type of end caps lining the ends of your cylindrical habitat.

  • Cost is dependent on both end cap type and habitat radius.

Habitat Stability

Return resources in exchange for a chance of catastrophic structural failure.

  • Habitats that end this phase with 100% Habitat Stability are guaranteed to survive.
  • Habitats that end this phase with <95% Habitat Stability will have their survival determined by probabilistic means.
  • Habitats that end this phase with 0% Habitat Stability will automatically be destroyed.

Terrain Modules

Land-Water Balance

Increase or decrease the proportional amount of water in the habitat area.

  • 0% completely removes permanent surface water from the entire habitat area. Surface water is only available after rainfall in ephemeral pools.
  • 100% completely inundates the entire habitat area. The complete absence of land is akin to an open ocean or a large lake.

Water Turbidity

Improve the maximum clarity of water to increase the maximum penetrative depth of sunlight and underwater visibility. Water bodies can be less clear than the value chosen if desired. Turbidity coefficients correspond with the following scenarios:

  • 10 — muddy water
  • 2 — murky water
  • 0.5 — turbid lake
  • 0.1 — coastal ocean
  • 0.05 — clear tropical ocean
  • 0.02 — very clear ocean
